Steve Job's life and the lessons and legacy he left behind

Lessons:
Anticipate and create the need Focus on design Master your communication

Life:
His father was Abdulfattah “John” Jandali and mother Joanne Schieble, but they put him up for adoption while he was not named (he only found out when he was 27) Was adopted by Clara and Paul Jobs His father taught him about electronics He even picked up a summer job with HP after calling company co-founder Bill Hewlett to ask for parts for a frequency counter he was building. It was at HP that a teenaged Jobs met his future partner and cofounder of Apple Computer Steve Wozniak. In 1974, Jobs took a position as a video game designer with Atari. At 21, Jobs and Wozniak sold a Volkswagen bus and scientific calculator to fund the company Wozniak made the computers and Jobs did marketing Apple I made $774,000 and Apple 2, $139 million 1982, became publicly traded and worth 1.2 billion Jobs got John Sculley of Pepsi-Cola, a marketing expert to take over as CEO Sculley got executives to push Jobs out of Apple Jobs left apple in 1985, spent 12 million to make NeXT Inc., didn’t do too well, and Apple bought it in 1996 for 429 million In 1997, he became the Apple CEO again Sister Mona Simpson wrote that just before dying, Jobs looked for a long time at his sister, Patty, then his wife and children, then passed them, and said his last words: “Oh wow. Oh wow. Oh wow.” Got pancreatic cancer and died after a couple years

Legacy:
Steve Jobs left us with the unforgettable and massive international technology company Apple. Through his invovlemetn and the effects of this company, our world has become so much mroe innovative. Jobs transformed the way we approach technology. He was a massive reason in why technology is such a big and influential part of modern lives. It started with simple personal computers and music players, now his company links the world together and provides platforms for the communication and programming that runs the world. He also left us with the many great animations from another company which he made into a global senstation, Pixar.
